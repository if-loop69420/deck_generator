The canonical baseof a vector space [latex]\begin{displaymath}V\end{displaymath}[/latex] is a subset [latex]\begin{displaymath}B\subset V\end{displaymath}[/latex], that is not only a base of [latex]\begin{displaymath}V\end{displaymath}[/latex], but also has the following structure (each column is a vector):

[latex]\begin{displaymath}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & ... & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & ... & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & ... & 1 \end{pmatrix}\end{displaymath}[/latex].
